FIRST.- PRELIMINARY MATTERS. Despite having been dismissed orally by the Court, after due deliberation, the violations of rights, arguing them at the hearing or referring to the order of November 9, 2023 of this Chamber or others, we will make a brief reference to some of the preliminary matters raised by the defense of Mr. Alves regarding the nullity of proceedings in relation to the cited order, whose argumentation we will collect substantially to facilitate any appeal that the parties may file against this judgment and the understanding and knowledge of the superior court about what was decided.

1.- Regarding the first preliminary matter, focused on the violation of the right to a process with all guarantees, the defense indicates that from the first moment the defendant was identified, so in accordance with article 284.1 of the Criminal Procedure Law the judicial authority should have been immediately informed of the crime and presumed perpetrator and pursuant to article 118 of the same legal text Mr. Alves should have been informed so he could exercise the right to defense from the first moment. And on December 31, 2022, the Court of Instruction issued an order of provisional dismissal. It indicates that if proceedings had been brought against him that same day, he could have submitted to, for example, a blood alcohol test.

Subsequently, the defendant would have had this right violated upon the Court receiving the supplementary police report and only ordering the reopening of proceedings, "delaying knowledge and communication of the pending criminal process to my client." Nor would the Court have done so when on January 16, 2023 it received the supplementary police report containing CDs with more than 20 hours of recordings.

2.- Regarding the denial of means of evidence, due to the intervention of the defense expert Dr. Blanca Navarro in the forensic medical examination of the victim, we already ruled in our order admitting evidence dated December 18, 2023 where we said that: There is no place for new psychiatric expert examination of the complainant with examination by Drs. Blanca Navarro and Jaime Ayguade. And this without prejudice to the experts issuing such report as they consider appropriate in view of the documentation in the proceedings.

On the one hand, the Statute of the Victim of Crime in its article 21 limits medical examinations of victims, which must be reduced to a minimum. And on the other hand, it does not appear that the defense expert was prevented, in accordance with what was established by Section 3 of this Provincial Court in its order of May 3, 2023, from accessing the examination carried out by the forensic doctors. In this regard, there is the order of May 23, 2023 where it was indicated that the complainant could be accompanied by the psychologist Silvia Moreira and the expert designated by the defense could attend when she was examined on June 13, 2023. And on July 12, 2023, a brief was received by the Court of Instruction presented by the defense of Mr. Alves in which it expressly stated "that the last investigative procedure was the forensic examination of the complainant by the IMLCFC on June 13, 2023. The passage of a month without the corresponding forensic medical report having been issued and without another investigative procedure pending is causing unreasonable procedural delay..."

Neither was the attendance of the defense expert denied, nor did the defense of Mr. Alves indicate this point in his brief of July 12.

In fact, at the trial Dr. Navarro testified, stating that she was present at the psychological examination together with Dr. Mateu. The forensic medical examination had already been performed. She also stated that she could not properly ask what she would have wished, so as not to have a conflict in front of the victim with the psychologist Dr. Mateu. And although she brought these facts to the attention of the defense attorney at the time, he was satisfied since, as stated, he requested that the report be obtained from the forensic doctor, without ever requesting a new expert examination or expansion of Dr. Navarro's powers in the examination. We understand that there has been no violation of the right to defense of Mr. Alves nor has the evidence been carried out irregularly, so the dismissal of this preliminary matter is appropriate.

3.- Regarding the existence of a parallel trial, we also referred to it in the order of November 9, 2023: "This Court cannot but admit that the principle of presumption of innocence protects any person, even in the extra-procedural sphere, as recognized by the Constitutional Court and the European Court of Human Rights. This extra-procedural dimension prevents treating as guilty someone who has not been so declared after a prior fair trial or casting doubt on someone who has been acquitted by final judgment.

Criminal proceedings affecting public figures have undoubted media repercussions. And frequently the facts are debated in different media, including analysis of evidence, and opinions are expressed that may violate the principle of presumption of innocence in its extra-procedural aspect, even though they do not come from authorities or public officials.

However, these attacks on the presumption of innocence do not have intra-procedural consequences, but must be prevented or, as the case may be, repaired from the sphere of protection of the right to honor. In no way can they mean the nullity of everything done in the proceedings.

Regarding the leaks that have existed throughout the processing of the case, this Court regrets them. But it regrets them as much in this case as in all cases. And likewise regrets the existence of so-called 'parallel trials.' But this does not mean the nullity of the investigation, nor is the procedure under article 627 of the Criminal Procedure Law the mechanism through which these leaks should be corrected, as they have no direct impact on the case."

These reflections made in the procedure under article 627 of the Criminal Procedure Law are maintained at this time, especially considering that, by virtue of the documents provided by the private prosecution at the beginning of the oral trial hearing, the existence of a parallel trial as well as leaks could also be attributed to the defendant's own circle. In any case, they have no bearing on the violation of the principle of presumption of innocence at a procedural level.

JUDGMENT

We CONVICT D. ALVES DA SILVA as criminally responsible author of a crime of rape under articles 178 and 179 of the Criminal Code, with the mitigating circumstance of reparation of damage under article 21.5 of the Criminal Code applying, to the penalty of FOUR YEARS AND SIX MONTHS of imprisonment, with the legal accessory of special disqualification for the right of passive suffrage during the time of the sentence, as well as the penalty of SUPERVISED RELEASE for a period of FIVE YEARS, which must be executed after completion of the prison sentence in accordance with article 192.1 of the Criminal Code.

In terms of civil liability, the defendant shall compensate VVVVV in the amount of ONE HUNDRED FIFTY THOUSAND EUROS for the moral damage suffered and injuries caused. Said amounts shall accrue legal interest increased by two points in accordance with Art. 576 of the Civil Procedure Law.

WE CONVICT D. ALVES DA SILVA as criminally responsible author of a minor crime of injuries to the penalty of 2 months' fine with a daily rate of 150 euros, with the subsidiary personal liability in case of non-payment under article 53 of the Criminal Code.

For compliance with the sentence imposed, all time that the defendant has been deprived of liberty for this cause is declared applicable and must be credited, provided it has not been credited in any other case.

The defendant is sentenced to pay the procedural costs, including those of the private prosecution.
